Kayan Panel | Tinjar River

The Kayan Panel refers to a traditional piece of artwork or carving created by the Kayan people, an indigenous group from Borneo, particularly in Sarawak, Malaysia. Known for their intricate craftsmanship, the Kayan’s carvings often depict spiritual and cultural themes, representing their deep connection to nature, ancestral heritage, and beliefs. These panels are important cultural symbols passed down through generations.

The Tinjar River is a significant waterway in Sarawak, vital to the indigenous communities, including the Kayan. It has historically served as a transportation route and is integral to the daily life, survival, and spiritual practices of these communities.

Kayan Panel | Tinjar River likely represents an artistic or cultural piece that connects the Kayan people’s heritage with the river’s significance. The artwork may incorporate motifs inspired by the river, illustrating the strong bond between the Kayan people and their natural surroundings.

Jalan Tun Abang Haji Openg, 93400 Kuching, Sarawak.
