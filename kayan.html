Incised Figurative Bamboo Container
Kayan Peoples

The Incised Figurative Bamboo Container is a traditional item created by the Kayan people, an indigenous group from Borneo. This container is typically made from bamboo, a versatile and abundant material in the region, and is intricately carved or incised with figurative designs. These carvings often depict human or animal figures, as well as symbolic motifs that hold cultural or spiritual significance. The figurative designs may reflect the Kayan people's beliefs, heritage, and their deep connection to the natural world.
Used for storing various items, such as food, tools, or personal belongings, the incised bamboo container is not just a practical object but also an art form that showcases the Kayan people's craftsmanship. The incised figures serve as a means of expressing identity, storytelling, and cultural traditions, passed down through generations. The container is an example of how the Kayan people blend functionality with artistic expression, creating objects that are both useful and rich in cultural meaning.

Jalan Tun Abang Haji Openg, 93400 Kuching, Sarawak.
