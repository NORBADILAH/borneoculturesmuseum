Carved Figure for 1899 Peace Treaty between
Kenyah, Kayan & Klemantan Marudi

The Carved Figure for the 1899 Peace Treaty between Kenyah, Kayan, and Klemantan Marudi refers to a traditional sculpture created to commemorate the peace agreement signed between these three indigenous groups in Borneo. The Kenyah, Kayan, and Klemantan peoples, who had a history of conflict, came together in 1899 to establish peace in the Marudi region of Sarawak, Malaysia.

The carved figure serves as a symbolic representation of the treaty, embodying the cultural significance of unity and reconciliation among these groups, and it likely features intricate designs that reflect the values and heritage of the participating communities.

Jalan Tun Abang Haji Openg, 93400 Kuching, Sarawak.
