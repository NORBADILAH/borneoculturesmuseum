Kejaman Chief's Door

The Kejaman Chief's Door is a significant cultural artifact from the Kejaman people, an indigenous group from Borneo, particularly in the Malaysian state of Sarawak. This door is traditionally crafted and intricately carved, symbolizing the status and authority of the chief or leader of the Kejaman community. The carvings on the door often feature elaborate patterns, animal motifs, and symbolic elements that reflect the chief's power, lineage, and spiritual role in the community. The door not only serves as an entryway but as a representation of the chief's leadership and the sacred nature of the longhouse or communal dwelling.
In the context of the Kejaman people, the Chief's Door holds deep cultural significance. It is more than just a physical barrier; it is a symbol of protection, authority, and connection to ancestral spirits.
