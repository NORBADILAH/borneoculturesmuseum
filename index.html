The History of
Borneo Cultures Museum

The Borneo Cultures Museum in Kuching, Sarawak, Malaysia, opened in March 2022, replacing the historic Sarawak Museum, which dates back to 1891.
The original museum, one of Southeast Asia's oldest, played a vital role in preserving the region's natural history and cultural artifacts. Construction of the new museum began in 2014 and was completed in 2020, but its opening was delayed by the COVID-19 pandemic.
Designed to resemble a bamboo shoot, the museum symbolizes growth and resilience, reflecting Sarawakâ€™s deep connection to nature and tradition. Today, it showcases Sarawak's indigenous heritage, biodiversity, and archaeological findings, serving as a cultural hub for visitors worldwide.
