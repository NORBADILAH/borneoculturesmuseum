Iban House Door

An Iban House Door with a Lizard is a traditional wooden door created by the Iban people of Borneo, known for their intricate and symbolic wood carvings. The carvings on the door often reflect the Iban’s rich cultural and spiritual beliefs. The lizard, in particular, is a significant motif in Iban art, symbolizing protection, good luck, and spiritual strength. It is believed that the lizard carving helps to ward off evil spirits, providing a safeguard for the home and its inhabitants.
The Iban people have a deep connection to nature, and the presence of animals like the lizard in their carvings reflects this relationship. The door, which serves as both an entryway and a cultural artifact, showcases the Iban’s craftsmanship while embodying their spiritual beliefs. The detailed carvings are not only functional but also serve to communicate important aspects of Iban culture, ensuring that the door is both a protective charm and a symbol of their traditions.

Jalan Tun Abang Haji Openg, 93400 Kuching, Sarawak.
